Understanding Empty Property Rates in 2025: A Complete Guide

By VacatAd Team • October 13, 2025 • 8 min read
← Back to Blog

Empty property rates are one of the most significant ongoing costs for commercial property owners. If you own a vacant commercial property in England or Wales, understanding how these rates work—and how to legally reduce them—can save you thousands of pounds annually.

What Are Empty Property Rates?

Business rates are a tax on commercial properties, calculated based on the property's rateable value as determined by the Valuation Office Agency (VOA). When a property becomes empty, owners are initially granted a relief period, but this doesn't last forever.

Key Point: After the initial relief period expires, property owners become liable for 100% of the business rates—even though the property is generating no income.

The Empty Property Relief Period

Most commercial properties qualify for an initial empty property relief period of three months (industrial properties get six months). During this time, you pay no business rates.

However, once this period expires:

  • You become liable for the full business rates amount
  • Rates continue to accrue monthly until the property is occupied
  • For a property with a £100,000 rateable value, this could mean £50,000+ per year in rates
  • These costs continue regardless of whether you're actively trying to let the property

Why Empty Property Rates Are So Costly

The impact of empty property rates can be devastating to your bottom line:

1. No Income, Full Tax

You're paying 100% of business rates while receiving 0% rental income. This double hit can quickly erode property values and investment returns.

2. Multiplier Effect

Business rates are calculated using a multiplier (currently around 49.9p for standard properties). As properties stay empty longer, the cumulative cost grows substantially.

3. No Automatic Relief

Unlike some other property taxes, there's no reduction for hardship or market conditions. Empty means full rates, regardless of circumstances.

Legal Ways to Reduce Empty Property Rates

While empty property rates are mandatory, there are legitimate, compliant strategies to manage this liability:

1. Beneficial Occupation

The most effective approach is creating genuine occupation through beneficial occupation. This involves:

  • Installing compliant technology and systems in the property
  • Creating genuine business activity that qualifies as occupation
  • Resetting the empty property relief period legally
  • Maintaining full compliance with VOA requirements

2. Short-Term Letting

Even temporary tenants can reset the relief period. However, this comes with challenges:

  • Finding suitable short-term tenants
  • Managing handover logistics
  • Potential property damage or disputes
  • Administrative overhead

3. Property Redevelopment

Properties undergoing structural renovation may qualify for exemption, but this requires:

  • Substantial, documented renovation work
  • Proper planning permissions
  • Significant capital investment

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many property owners make costly errors when dealing with empty property rates:

  1. Waiting too long: The longer you wait to address rates liability, the more you lose
  2. Non-compliant schemes: Some "solutions" don't meet VOA requirements and can result in back-charges
  3. Ignoring the problem: Rates don't go away—they accumulate and become a mounting liability
  4. Inadequate documentation: Proper record-keeping is essential for any rates relief strategy
