Why 2025 Is the Year Technology Redefined Business Rates Mitigation

By: VacatAd Team Published: 18 June 2025 Read time: 7 min

Across the UK, property owners are facing increasing scrutiny from local authorities as business rates reforms continue to tighten enforcement. Traditional mitigation schemes — such as temporary storage or nominal tenancies — are now more likely to face challenge. In this environment, technology has become the key to maintaining compliance while still reducing costs.

VacatAd's approach exemplifies how the next generation of property management solutions use smart technology, automation, and data to deliver genuine beneficial occupation — and verifiable evidence to back it up.

The end of old-fashioned mitigation

For years, many property owners relied on storage-based or paper-lease strategies to qualify as "occupied" for rating purposes. But following the 48th Street Holdings Ltd and Principled Offsite Logistics Ltd [2025] EWHC 1130 (KB) ruling, the courts reaffirmed that occupation must align with the statutory purpose of rates mitigation — not avoidance.

That means local authorities now expect to see:

  • A real use of the property, not symbolic occupation.
  • Proof of activity and presence during the occupation period.
  • Documentation showing that the property was capable of beneficial occupation and that the use delivered some tangible public or economic value.

Bottom line: Schemes that rely on artificial tenancies or empty storage are out of step with current case law.

Why tech solutions are now essential

Modern compliance depends on verifiable, data-driven evidence. VacatAd's Wi-Fi-based occupation system provides exactly that — a digital footprint of continuous use.

  • Live connectivity logs: Demonstrate that the property is active and occupied, showing uptime and user connections every 15 minutes.
  • Motion-sensor integration: Provides real-time evidence of human presence or maintenance visits.
  • Cloud monitoring and reports: Generate automated compliance documentation suitable for local authority queries or appeals.

This means landlords don't just claim beneficial occupation — they can prove it.

Lower risk, higher transparency

Authorities are becoming increasingly vigilant in identifying contrived arrangements. In contrast, VacatAd's technology is intentionally transparent:

  • Occupation is visible and measurable.
  • The property provides a social utility — public Wi-Fi access and free advertising for local businesses.
  • The process aligns perfectly with the principle of lawful beneficial use confirmed by the courts.

By grounding the occupation in demonstrable, technology-enabled activity, VacatAd reduces legal risk and reinforces credibility with both councils and rating surveyors.
